HTWK-App Project

In scope of my studies as a computer scientist I have started the HTWK-App as an open-source project. It evolved fastly into an outstanding project, developed by five people. The HTWK-App is a website shaped mobile application that is published as a WebApp. It provides different services from the Leipzig University of Applied Sciences, like canteen offers, student grades, a list of employees and much more.

I have worked at the mobile application and used HTML 5, CSS 3 and Javascript. As frameworks, we decided to use JQuery, JQuery Mobile and Apache Cordova. In addition I have become the project leader and temporarily managed up to four developers. Currently, I keep working at this project in the position just mentioned.

  • Developed for: Leipzig University of Applied Sciences
  • Period: October 2013 until today
  • Website: HTWK-App

Linux - A Developers Operating System

Since 2010 I have been using Linux. I have started with Ubuntu as a system to program C, but I have switched systems completely in a short period of time. Especially the surfaces of Linux got concise advantages (virtual desktops, comfortable consoles and much more) over the Windows surface. In the end of 2012, I have switched to Fedora. They have a better open-source philosophy and their repositories are way more up to date than the ones from Ubuntu. Fedora is my operating system for daily usage, regardless of multimedia or developer system.

Through the many years of Linux usage I am familiar with the systems of Ubuntu and Fedora, representing THE two major distributions. I have dealt with the various surfaces, package managers, Linux subsystems and many other units.

Embedded Systems

Ever since I can think I was interested in small devices and there hidden abilities. As a result of this, I attended many embedded system courses at the university. I learned from discrete and integrated circuits, micro-controllers, bus-networks all the way up to ARM and DSP systems, like the Raspberry PI and the Cubieboard.

As part of my studies I developed a measuring unit for intelligent streetlights, consisting of an ARM SoC and some sensors that estimate weather conditions. During an internship I programmed an ARM-DSP Linux board in C and developed small applications with Texas Instruments micro-controllers.
